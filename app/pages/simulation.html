Why operators run simulations

The cost of finding out the hard way

Space-weather degradation is rare enough that few operators have a personal reference for what a Gannon-class storm does to their fleet. Simulation closes that gap before it becomes a real outage.

🛩️
Route stress-testing
UAV / BVLOS / aviation operators replay the storm against a planned mission to surface degradation windows and divert points before launch.
🌾
RTK exposure analysis
Precision-ag teams quantify how many hours of autosteer they'd lose during a Gannon-class storm — and what that costs at peak season.
🪖
Operator training
Run a unit through a real historical storm in the simulator: practice fallback comms, route diversion, INS handoff before the real event.
⛴️
Comms contingency planning
Maritime, SAR, and polar dispatch test which HF / SATCOM bands actually survive different storm classes for their operating area.
📊
Insurance / risk modelling
Quantify the expected outage cost of a once-per-decade storm against a portfolio of routes, fields, or assets — for risk-pricing or board sign-off.
🛰️
Customer pitches / TTX
Show prospects exactly what would have happened during May 2024 — over their fleet, on their routes — instead of generic NOAA charts.
Workflow

Three steps to a real answer

01 · Pick
Choose a scenario
Three real historical storms (May 2024 G5, Halloween 2003, St. Patrick's 2015) plus four showcase scenarios for comms, GPS, polar, and SATCOM degradation.
02 · Drop
Add your AOI / route
Drop waypoints, draw a region of interest, or pick a customer profile (defense, aerospace, commercial-grid) for a pre-tuned regional view.
03 · Scrub
See what would have happened
Hour-by-hour replay with translated outputs — meters of GPS error, minutes of HF blackout, severity per region. Export KMZ / CSV / GeoJSON.
Real archive scenarios

Three storms backfilled

No synthetic events. Each scenario uses the actual hour-cadence Bz, solar wind, Kp, and proton flux from the archive, plus reconstructed flare timelines.
