Defense · Forward-deployed
ATAK ground operations
What goes wrong A G3+ storm collapses HF propagation over the AOR. The team's primary comms goes silent without warning; SATCOM degrades minutes later.
What IonShield surfaces "HF 3–12 MHz blackout ~42 min over polar leg, GPS L1 +18 m at waypoints 2–3, switch to backup VHF / SATCOM-Ka." Live overlay in ATAK.
Operator decision Pre-authorise alternate comms before the window, reroute waypoint 3 east of the auroral oval, brief team on degraded GPS for INS handoff.
Cost avoidance Mission failure or compromised C2 during a degraded window — measured in lives, not dollars.

UAV · BVLOS · Drone
Beyond-visual-line-of-sight UAV ops
What goes wrong RTK GPS error climbs above the platform's 1.5 m threshold mid-flight. The aircraft loses precision lock, drifts, returns-to-launch or violates BVLOS waiver conditions.
What IonShield surfaces "GPS L1 expected to exceed 2 m over flight zone 14:20–16:40 UTC. Operate before 14:15 or after 16:55."
Operator decision Schedule the inspection / spray flight for the clean window. If the mission can't move, fly with manual oversight or swap the platform.
Cost avoidance ~$4–12k per aborted commercial UAV mission, plus regulatory exposure for BVLOS violations.

Precision agriculture · RTK
Autosteer + variable-rate applicators
What goes wrong RTK fix degrades during harvest or planting. Autosteer drops to float-fix or DGPS, autosteer accuracy drops from 2 cm to 30+ cm — overlap waste, missed strips, off-target chemistry.
What IonShield surfaces "RTK fix integrity at risk over the Plains tomorrow 10:00–14:00 local. Plan high-precision passes for morning window before storm onset."
Operator decision Push planting to the morning window, use the afternoon for grain handling / non-RTK tasks, hold off on variable-rate fertiliser passes.
Cost avoidance $5–15 / acre on a 1,500-acre operation in overlap and re-pass waste during a single bad day.

Aviation · Polar dispatch
Polar route GNSS integrity
What goes wrong Polar-cap absorption knocks out HF; GNSS RAIM goes unavailable mid-route; flight is rerouted to a southern track at fuel + crew cost.
What IonShield surfaces "Polar HF likely lost 18:00–22:00 UTC, GNSS integrity degraded at FL370+. 24-h advance forecast issued for dispatch desk."
Operator decision Pre-flight reroute decision (saves last-minute fuel uplift), or accept the polar route with backup HF and pre-authorised altitude change.
Cost avoidance $50–80k per polar diversion (fuel + slot + crew + fleet repositioning).

Maritime · SAR · Polar
Long-haul HF + SAR comms
What goes wrong Long-haul vessel loses HF without warning during a high-latitude transit. Search-and-rescue coordination depends on links that may not exist.
What IonShield surfaces "HF 8 MHz unusable for the next 4 h on the Northwest Passage; switch to Iridium SBD or wait until 02:30 UTC."
Operator decision Schedule comms windows around recovery; pre-authorise SBD spend; brief vessel master on the outage timeline.
Cost avoidance SAR response delays measured in hours, not minutes; missed ETA penalties, regulatory exposure.

Surveying · Construction
High-accuracy field surveying
What goes wrong A crew shows up to a site, sets up RTK, and can't hold fix. Day is lost; daily-rate equipment + crew burns money.
What IonShield surfaces "GPS L1 / L2 ionospheric error elevated at site coordinates today 13:00–17:00 local; survey-grade fix unlikely in window."
Operator decision Reschedule field crew for a clean day, or run the morning window only; do office / processing tasks during the bad window.
Cost avoidance $2–6k per lost field day on a typical RTK survey crew.

Autonomy · Off-road
Mining / agriculture / autonomous fleets
What goes wrong Driverless haul trucks or robotic tractors lose differential GPS, drop to manual takeover, and shed throughput across the entire site.
What IonShield surfaces "DGPS performance at site degraded for the next 3 h; expect ~14 m position uncertainty during peak. Recommend operator-takeover policy active.
Operator decision Activate manual-takeover policy ahead of the window (no surprise drops); shift crew schedule; pre-stage backup operators.
Cost avoidance 5–10% of daily haul throughput on an autonomous mine = six-figure / shift.

Satellite · SATCOM operations
Link-margin scheduling
What goes wrong An ionospheric scintillation window eats 4 dB of link margin during a planned uplink; the file transfer fails and slot has to be rebooked.
What IonShield surfaces "L-band fade peak 3.2 dB at 18:40 UTC over equatorial anomaly belt. Reschedule uplink before 18:15 or after 19:30."
Operator decision Reschedule uplink, switch to a backup band, or accept the loss with retry budget.
Cost avoidance Re-booked slot fees + commercial penalties for missed delivery windows.
