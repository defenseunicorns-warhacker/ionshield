Scenario 1: Defense Mission Planning

Special Operations / Ground Maneuver · UAV / UAS Routing

⚠ Pain Point

A SOCOM element is planning a 6-waypoint vehicle route through mountainous terrain. The S6 knows that ionospheric disturbances degrade Blue Force Tracking GPS and can make HF radio checkpoints unreliable, but has no tool to quantify the risk for specific waypoints or recommend a comms fallback plan. A generic NOAA Kp alert says "unsettled conditions" — useless without route context.

✓ IonShield Solution

The operator loads the 6-waypoint route into IonShield, selects "MRAP" platform (GPS L1L2, criticality 4). Within 200 ms, each route segment is color-coded: green for WP01–WP03, orange for WP04–WP05, red for WP06. The decision reads: "CAUTION — GPS error exceeds 35 m at WP05–WP06 due to auroral oval incursion. Recommend enabling INS coupling before WP04 and confirming SATCOM link before departure." Confidence: HIGH (0.84).

Scenario 2: Polar Aviation Routing

Commercial / Military Aviation · BVLOS UAS Operations

⚠ Pain Point

A polar transatlantic flight (JFK → LHR) crosses the auroral zone during an active Kp=6 event. HF radio is required as a backup communications channel above 60°N, but polar cap absorption can make HF completely unusable for hours. Current crew resources — NOAA SWPC text bulletins — give global alerts without route-specific HF viability or GPS error forecasts.

✓ IonShield Solution

Dispatch inputs the polar route (7 waypoints). IonShield identifies waypoints WP4–WP6 as inside the auroral oval (risk: DEGRADED) with GPS L1L5 error estimated at 28–45 m. HF viability is marked false for WP5–WP6 (polar cap absorption active). The decision: "CAUTION — HF NOT VIABLE at WP5–WP6. Confirm SATCOM L-band backup. GPS integrity monitoring is recommended from WP4 onward." The 72-hour forecast timeline shows conditions improving within 4 hours — a 2-hour departure delay yields a full GO verdict.

Scenario 3: Satellite Comms Scheduling

Satellite Ground Stations · MILSATCOM · Commercial LEO/GEO

⚠ Pain Point

A satellite ground station schedules critical payload uplinks during a geomagnetic storm. Scintillation and increased ionospheric delay cause bit-error rate spikes that corrupt uplink commands, requiring costly retransmission windows and mission schedule slippage. The operations team lacks an automated tool to flag high-scintillation periods before scheduling.

✓ IonShield Solution

IonShield's comms decision endpoint (GET /api/v2/comms-decision) is polled by the scheduling system every 5 minutes. During elevated Kp periods, the verdict switches from USE_PRIMARY_HF to SWITCH_TO_SATCOM — automatically triggering the scheduler to defer L-band uplinks to the next quiet window identified in the 72-hour forecast. After-action replay confirms which uplink windows were affected and validates the scheduling logic.
