Capabilities

What IonShield does

The product is one thing: a translation layer between raw space weather and the answer your operators need. Below is what ships today, organised by the kind of decision it supports.

1 · The translation layer

Operator answers, not raw indices

The core promise of the product. Anyone can read a NOAA Kp number; almost no one can act on it. IonShield converts each driver into the number an operator cares about.

For navigation
GPS error in meters
Per-region L1, L1L2, and L1L5 position error you'll actually see — derived from Klobuchar + Mannucci ionospheric models against the live TEC grid.
Output · e.g. "GPS L1 +18.4 m over polar leg, NOMINAL elsewhere"
For comms
HF outage duration
CCIR-888 absorption + Bailey polar-cap model, scaled by current X-ray flux and proton events. Output is minutes-of-blackout, not nebulous "degraded" labels.
Output · e.g. "HF 3-12 MHz blackout ~42 min over high latitudes"
For SATCOM
Link-margin loss in dB
Nakagami-m fading model on L-band terminals; tells you whether your link margin survives the next scintillation window or whether to reschedule.
Output · e.g. "SATCOM L-band fade 3.2 dB peak at 18:40 UTC"
For routing
Per-segment go / advisory / no-go
Drop a route or AOI; each segment gets a typed verdict, the underlying score, and the alternate route or backup nav option to take.
Output · e.g. "Leg 2: ADVISORY, switch to backup INS · Leg 3: NO-GO until 22:00"
2 · Live awareness

Regional risk, updated every 5 minutes

A 324-cell global grid (10° lat × 20° lon) re-scores against fresh NOAA + GloTEC data every five minutes. The 3D dashboard surfaces the result.

3D risk globe
CesiumJS globe with click-to-inspect regions, route waypoints, and severity-coloured cells. Drives the operator view.
Who uses it · ATAK ops, dispatch desks, surveyors
Route + waypoint analysis
Click anywhere on the globe to add a waypoint; the worst-leg verdict and per-system impact appear instantly.
Who uses it · UAV / BVLOS, polar aviation, mission planners
Replay drawer
Scrub the last 30 days of archived snapshots to compare against live state, debug an outage, or train an operator.
Who uses it · post-mission analysts, training cells, operations supervisors
3 · Simulation & scenario testing

Stress-test plans against
real historical storms

Eight scenarios ship with the product, including the May 2024 G5 (Gannon), Halloween 2003, and St. Patrick's Day 2015 storms — backfilled with real NASA OMNI archive data, not synthetic.

Real archive scenarios
Replay any of three real G4–G5 storms with hour-by-hour fidelity. Drop your route on the map and see what would have happened.
Customer demo sandbox
Pick a region and time window, generate the impact picture for that AOI. Lets prospects answer "what would this have cost us?"
Operator-ready exports
Each scenario downloads as KMZ (Google Earth, ATAK), Earth Studio CSV (animators), or time-indexed GeoJSON (QGIS, Mapbox).

5 · ML & learning

Auditable models today,
customer-tuned learning next

Every ML decision is traceable. Every model can be retrained against an individual customer's environment.

Today
24-hour Kp forecaster
Ridge model trained on the NASA OMNI archive + live snapshots; predicts +1, +3, +6, +12, +24 h Kp with G-scale severity.
Today
Champion / challenger A/B
New models register as shadow challengers; auto-promote when they beat the champion on real operator feedback.
Near-term
Per-region heads
One specialised model per geomagnetic-latitude band so polar regions and the equatorial anomaly get the precision they need.
Near-term
Customer feedback loops
Ground-truth predictions against the customer's own GPS C/N0, RTK fix-time, or HF-link logs; the model learns the operator's environment.
Direction
Mission-history-informed recommendations
As accumulated mission and simulation data grows, the system learns which decisions worked and surfaces better recommendations next time.
Direction
Decision-support that improves over time
The long-term arc is autonomy-friendly recommendations — IonShield suggests, the operator (or the system, if cleared) acts.
