Roadmap

The honest version of the arc

Direction we are committed to, with a clear separation between what ships today, what's near-term engineering, and what's the larger product vision.

Today · live
Auditable models in production

Two ML systems run live in the public deployment. Pure-Python implementations, JSON-readable weights, full audit trail.

  • Event classifier — multinomial logistic regression over 5 storm classes (background, geomag main, SEP event, M-flare, X-flare).
  • 24-hour Kp forecaster — multi-horizon ridge model trained on the NASA OMNI archive.
  • Champion / challenger A/B with auto-promotion when a new model beats live performance.
Near · 1–3 mo
Customer-tuned model versions

The headline lift: each customer's deployment grows its own model tuned to its own operating environment. No new science required — just the engineering to wire ground-truth in.

  • Customer feedback ground-truth — pipe RTK fix-time, GPS C/N0, HF SNR, and mission outcomes into the feedback store. The model is scored against the operator's actual experience, not a benchmark.
  • Per-region heads — one specialised forecaster per geomagnetic-latitude band (polar, mid-lat, equatorial anomaly).
  • Calibrated probabilities — Platt / isotonic scaling so a 70% blackout probability is empirically 70%.
Direction · 6–12 mo
Autonomous decision support

The long arc. A customer-specific model with enough mission and simulation history starts surfacing decisions, not just forecasts. Operator stays in the loop until trust is earned.

  • Mission-history-informed recommendations — accumulated outcomes tell the system which decisions worked, under which conditions, for which assets.
  • Storm-phase awareness — the model says "wait 90 min for recovery," not just "monitor."
  • Autonomous suggestions on cleared workflows — for routine, low-criticality decisions (e.g. "shift the harvest pass to morning"), the model can act with operator-defined authorisation. Defense and high-criticality always stays human-in-loop.
How the loop closes

A feedback loop, not a black box

Every prediction logs its inputs. Every operator outcome becomes a label. The next model is trained on what actually happened to your fleet — and proves itself in shadow before going live.

1

Ingest

Public NOAA + NASA feeds combine with the customer's own ground-truth (GPS C/N0, RTK fix-time, HF SNR, mission outcomes) — inside the customer's perimeter when required.

2

Predict

The active champion model issues predictions on every operator request. Each prediction is logged with its inputs and the operator-visible verdict.

3

Compare

Operator outcomes (was the link viable? did the RTK actually hold?) come back as labels. Drift metrics surface when the model loses track of reality.

4

Retrain

A new model trains on the updated dataset, registers as a shadow challenger, and runs alongside the champion on real traffic — no production risk while we evaluate.

5

Promote

When the challenger beats the champion across a sample window with margin, the system auto-swaps. Promotion is logged; rollback is one API call.

6

Compound

Over months, your model converges on your environment, asset mix, and risk tolerance. The same foundation, your outcome history — your team's compounding advantage.

Why auditable

For operators who have to
defend every recommendation

Defense, regulated commercial, anyone who has to answer "why did the system tell you to do that?" — auditable matters more than sophisticated. We optimise for the ability to read the model.

Readable model weights

Pure-Python regressors with JSON weights you can print to a screen. Compliance reviewers can read the model.

Shadow mode before promote

No model ships to production users without proving itself on real traffic alongside the champion. Promotion is reversible.

Physics as the floor

When the ML disagrees with physics-grounded models, physics wins by default. The ML adds, doesn't replace.

Operator outcomes drive learning

Your team's outcomes are first-class training signal. The model gets better at your operator's job — not at a benchmark leaderboard.
